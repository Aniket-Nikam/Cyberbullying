For Witnesses

The Bystander Action Guide

If you witness cyberbullying, you have more power than you think. Here's a step-by-step guide.

01
👁️
Recognise it for what it is

If someone is being mocked, threatened, excluded, or humiliated online — even "as a joke" — that's cyberbullying. You don't need to be a victim to take action. Identifying it is the first step.

HarassmentMockingExclusionThreats
↓
02
🚫
Don't engage or amplify

Do not like, share, comment on, or laugh at content targeting someone. Every reaction feeds the cycle. Leaving the group chat or unfollowing the thread removes your participation from the equation.

Don't ShareDon't ReactLeave the Thread
↓
03
📸
Document the evidence

Screenshot the harmful content before it disappears — including timestamps and usernames. This evidence can be reported to the platform, school, or authorities. Use our Evidence Upload to store it securely.

ScreenshotSave TimestampNote Username
↓
04
🤝
Support the target privately

Reach out to the person being bullied privately — not publicly. Let them know you saw it, you believe them, and you're on their side. Often, knowing someone cares is the most powerful thing a bystander can do.

Private MessageShow Support"I Believe You"
↓
05
🚨
Report it — on the platform and beyond

Use the platform's built-in report tools to flag the content. If there's a real threat involved, report to the school or call 1930. You can file a report on this site anonymously without giving your name.

Report to PlatformTell a TeacherCall 1930

How-To Guides

Report on Every Platform

Step-by-step instructions to block, report, and screenshot evidence on every major platform.

01
Screenshot the Evidence

Press Home + Power (iPhone) or Volume Down + Power (Android) simultaneously. Do this BEFORE blocking or reporting.

Phone buttons → Screenshot saved to Gallery
02
Block the Account

Visit their profile → tap the three-dot menu (⋯) in the top right → select "Block" → confirm. They will not be notified.

Profile → ⋯ → Block → Block Account
03
Report the Post or Message

Tap and hold the message or tap ⋯ on a post → "Report" → choose "Bullying or Harassment" → follow prompts. Reports are reviewed by Instagram's team.

Message → Hold → Report → Bullying or Harassment
04
Restrict the Account

Restricting is softer than blocking — the person can't see when you're active or if you've read their messages. Good for known classmates.

Profile → ⋯ → Restrict
01
Screenshot Harmful Messages

Take screenshots of abusive messages showing sender name, timestamp, and content. WhatsApp shows timestamps on individual messages — tap them to confirm.

Message → Tap → Info → Screenshot
02
Block the Contact

Open chat → tap their name at top → scroll down → "Block". For groups: Open group → tap group name → "Exit Group", then block the admin or members.

Chat → Name → Block Contact
03
Report to WhatsApp

When you block, WhatsApp will ask if you want to report the contact. Tap "Report and Block". This sends recent messages to WhatsApp's team for review.

Block → "Report and Block"
04
Report a Group

If a group chat is used to bully: Open group → tap group name → scroll to bottom → "Report Group". Exit the group afterward for your safety.

Group → Group Name → Report Group → Exit Group
01
Save Evidence Quickly

Snaps disappear — use your phone's screenshot IMMEDIATELY before they vanish. Snapchat may notify the sender, but the evidence is more important.

Screenshot immediately → Saved to Camera Roll
02
Block the User

Go to their profile by tapping their username → tap ⋯ → "Block". They will be removed from your friends list and cannot contact you.

Profile → ⋯ → Block
03
Report the User

Visit their profile → tap ⋯ → "Report" → choose relevant category (Harassment, Threats, Bullying). Add details for a faster response.

Profile → ⋯ → Report → Harassment
04
Report a Story or Snap

Press and hold on the story or snap → tap the flag icon → select reason → submit. Snapchat reviews all reports for safety violations.

Hold Snap/Story → 🚩 → Submit Report
01
Report a Harmful Comment

Long-press the comment → tap "Report" → select "Bullying and harassment". TikTok has a dedicated anti-bullying team that reviews reports.

Comment → Long Press → Report → Bullying
02
Report a Video

Tap the Share arrow (➤) on the video → "Report" → select relevant violation. For videos targeting someone specifically, choose "Bullying and harassment".

Video → Share (➤) → Report → Category
03
Block & Filter Comments

Visit profile → tap ⋯ → "Block". You can also use Comment Filters (Settings → Privacy → Comments) to automatically hide harmful keywords.

Profile → ⋯ → Block | Settings → Privacy → Comment Filters
04
Report Account to TikTok

Visit profile → tap ⋯ → "Report" → "Pretending to be someone" or "Bullying and harassment". Serious cases are escalated to law enforcement if needed.

Profile → ⋯ → Report → Bullying
01
Copy Message Link for Evidence

Right-click (or hold on mobile) any message → "Copy Message Link". This creates a permanent URL to the exact message — far better than a screenshot alone.

Right-click message → Copy Message Link
02
Block the User

Click their profile picture → click ⋯ → "Block". Blocked users cannot DM you or see your messages in shared servers.

Profile Picture → ⋯ → Block
03
Report to Discord

Right-click the message → "Report Message". Include the copied message link. For serious threats, email Discord's Trust & Safety: [email protected]

Right-click → Report Message | [email protected]
04
Report the Server

If a whole server is toxic or targeting you, go to Discord's report page: discord.com/safety. Provide the server invite link and message evidence.

discord.com/safety → Report a server

Awareness

Recognise the Warning Signs

If you notice these behaviours in yourself or someone you care about, reach out for help.

📱

Device Anxiety

Nervous around their phone, hiding screens, or seeming upset after using social media.

🚫

Social Withdrawal

Avoiding friends, skipping school or activities they used to enjoy, isolating themselves.

😔

Mood Changes

Sudden sadness, anger, irritability, or emotional outbursts after being online.

😴

Sleep Disruption

Trouble sleeping, nightmares, or staying up late obsessively checking devices.

📚

Declining Grades

Drop in academic performance, lack of focus, or not wanting to attend school.

💔

Low Self-Esteem

Talking negatively about themselves, feeling worthless, or expressing hopelessness.

Action Steps

What You Can Do

Whether you're being bullied, you witnessed it, or you're a parent — here's how to take action.

01

Document Everything

Screenshot messages, posts, and threats. Save timestamps and URLs. Use our Evidence Upload above to store it securely — you won't need an account.

02

Block & Report

Use platform tools to block the bully and report abuse. Most social media sites have built-in reporting for harassment — use it every time.

03

Tell Someone You Trust

Talk to a parent, teacher, counsellor, or trusted adult. You don't have to face this alone — support is available around the clock.

For Adults

Parent & School Toolkit

Practical resources for parents, teachers, and school administrators to prevent and respond to cyberbullying.

💬
How to Start the Conversation
Most children won't come to you first. This guide covers non-threatening conversation starters, what NOT to say, and how to make your child feel safe coming to you.
  • Ask about their online life regularly, not just when something goes wrong
  • Never dismiss "it's just the internet" — online pain is real pain
  • Don't take devices away as punishment — it closes the communication door
🔍
Warning Signs Checklist
A printable one-page checklist of behavioural, emotional, and academic warning signs that your child may be experiencing cyberbullying.
  • Avoiding phone/devices after previously being glued to them
  • Emotional distress after using social media
  • Unexplained withdrawal from friends or activities
⚖️
Legal Action Guide for Parents
Know your rights. This guide explains which laws protect your child, how to file a complaint, and what to expect from the police and court process.
  • IT Act 2000 — Sections 66, 66C, 67, 72
  • IPC Sections 499, 503, 506, 507, 509
  • Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act
🧠
Supporting Mental Health at Home
Cyberbullying leaves emotional scars. This resource helps parents support their child's mental health and know when to seek professional help.
  • Validate feelings without catastrophising
  • Maintain routines and offline activities
  • When to involve a professional counsellor
🎓
Digital Literacy Lesson Plan
A ready-to-use 45-minute lesson plan for grades 7–12 on cyberbullying awareness, digital empathy, and safe online behaviour.
  • Interactive group activity and discussion prompts
  • Real scenario role-play exercises
  • Assessment rubric included
📢
Anonymous Reporting Setup Guide
How to set up an anonymous reporting system in your school — from a simple suggestion box to a digital form — so students feel safe coming forward.
  • Free tools that work in any school
  • How to communicate the system to students
  • How to process and respond to reports ethically
🧑‍🏫
Staff Training Workshop
A 2-hour facilitated workshop plan to train teachers and counsellors to identify, respond to, and prevent cyberbullying in their classrooms.
  • Case studies from Indian school contexts
  • Intervention protocols and escalation paths
  • Parent communication templates
📊
School Climate Survey Template
A 15-question anonymous survey to measure cyberbullying prevalence in your school and identify at-risk groups. Includes analysis guidance.
  • Validated questions used in national research
  • Anonymous format to maximise honest responses
  • Suggested follow-up actions by severity level
📄
School Anti-Cyberbullying Policy Template
A comprehensive policy template that schools can adapt, covering definitions, prohibited behaviours, reporting procedures, and consequences.
  • Aligned with CBSE and ICSE guidelines
  • Roles and responsibilities clearly defined
  • Covers student, staff, and parent conduct
✉️
Parent Communication Templates
Ready-to-send email templates for notifying parents of a cyberbullying incident — whether their child is a victim, bystander, or perpetrator.
  • Victim notification with empathy-first language
  • Perpetrator family notification template
  • Witness/bystander parent communication
🤝
Student Acceptable Use Policy
A student-friendly agreement on responsible digital behaviour for school devices and online platforms. Designed to be signed at the start of each academic year.
  • Age-appropriate language for grades 6–12
  • Clear consequences section
  • Includes parent/guardian co-signature section
